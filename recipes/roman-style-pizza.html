Roman Style Pizza

Description

Roman style pizza, also know as pizza in teglia (teglia is italian for pan), is a rectangular pizza cooked in a pan that is commonly eaten as a street food

It's known for it's light, airy crust that is able to hold a substantial amount of toppings.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Yeast (or sourdough starter)
  • Crushed canned tomatoes
  • Cheese
  • Assorted toppings

Steps

  1. Mix together the first four ingredients to create the dough
  2. Every 30 minutes, stretch and fold the dough, until you have done so three times
  3. Blend the tomatoes lightly with some salt to create the sauce
  4. Once the dough has adequately risen, gently stretch it out into a rectangle, pressing it softly with the tips of your fingers to preserve the air bubbles within
  5. Top the dough with the sauce and par bake it for 10 minutes at 450
  6. Take the pizza out of the oven, top it with the cheese and other toppings, and then put it back in for 10 more minutes at 450
